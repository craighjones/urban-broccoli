Assessing end-to-end services

Overview

How do we assess an end-to-end service and its features, products or sub-services at a point in time; especially when the products/sub-services can be at different stages of development?

I began with mapping the end-to-end, defining a narrative and speaking with the services to validate my assumptions. Once I’d started the engagement I then developed a cross service community. The community meets fortnightly and is made up of designers, researchers, product and assurance, this is an opportunity to share updates, demo designs, drive consistency across the end-to-end and identify duplication of effort. I presented the approach and framework at Services Week 2020

Design sprints

Overview

These have been useful in educating the wider business about delivering outcomes not outputs and looking at critical business questions through design and user needs. The nature of design sprints are also extremely collaborative and embrace co-design as a core principle.

Design crits

Overview

It’s an opportunity to share work at any stage of the design process. It’s an opportunity to involve the wider team to help nurture design problems and identify dependencies across service teams. It’s an opportunity to ensure the products quality, consistency and identify new patterns.

I wanted to improve the engagement and environment by making them more collaborative, I defined clear roles; presenter, facilitator and audience as well as creating a set of principles. The principles underpin every crit and have improved the overall attendance and contribution. It’s now an enjoyable and learning experience.
